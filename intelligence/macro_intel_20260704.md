## 📉 Macroeconomic Summary & Trends
- **Energy Deflation:** The potential for lower energy costs via diplomacy may offset some of the sticky inflation seen in the US, potentially giving the Fed more room for growth preservation.
- **Compute as State Power:** Compute is no longer just a commodity but is being treated as a strategic national asset, mirroring the "arms race" dynamics of the 20th century.
- **Trade Fragmentation:** The move toward "trusted trade" and aggressive tariffs is creating a fragmented global economy, increasing the appeal of crypto as a neutral, cross-border settlement layer.
